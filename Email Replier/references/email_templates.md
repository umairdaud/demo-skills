# Email Templates and Examples

## Business Response Templates

### Standard Business Reply
```
Subject: Re: [Original Subject]

Dear [Name],

Thank you for your email regarding [topic]. I appreciate you reaching out.

[Address main points from the email]

[Provide any necessary information or next steps]

Please let me know if you have any questions or need additional information.

Best regards,
[Your name]
```

### Customer Service Response
```
Subject: Re: [Original Subject]

Dear [Customer Name],

Thank you for contacting us about [issue]. We appreciate your patience as we address your concern.

[Explain solution or steps being taken]

[Provide timeline if applicable]

If you have any further questions, please don't hesitate to reach out.

Best regards,
[Support Team]
```

### Follow-up Email
```
Subject: Following up on [original topic]

Hi [Name],

I wanted to follow up on our previous email/conversation about [topic] on [date].

[Provide update or ask for status]

[Include specific next steps or deadline if applicable]

I look forward to hearing from you soon.

Best regards,
[Your name]
```

## Tone-Specific Examples

### Formal Tone
- Use complete sentences and proper grammar
- Avoid contractions
- Address people by their titles (Mr., Ms., Dr.)
- Example: "We appreciate your inquiry and will respond promptly."

### Professional but Casual Tone
- Can use contractions
- May use first names if appropriate
- Still maintain respect and clarity
- Example: "Thanks for your message. We'll get back to you soon."

### Friendly/Collegial Tone
- Can use casual language appropriate to the relationship
- May include light pleasantries
- Still maintain professionalism
- Example: "Thanks for checking in! Here's the update you requested."

## Common Email Scenarios

### Apology Email
1. Acknowledge the issue immediately
2. Express genuine regret
3. Explain what happened (briefly)
4. Outline steps to fix the issue
5. Prevent future occurrences

### Information Request
1. State the purpose clearly
2. Specify what information is needed
3. Provide context for why it's needed
4. Give a reasonable deadline
5. Offer to clarify if needed

### Meeting Follow-up
1. Thank attendees for their time
2. Summarize key decisions made
3. List action items with owners
4. Confirm next meeting date if applicable
5. Provide contact for questions
