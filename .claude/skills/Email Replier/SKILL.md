# Copywriting Email Replier

This skill provides guidance for crafting effective email replies with appropriate tone, structure, and content.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when helping users compose email replies that need to be professional, clear, and achieve specific communication goals. This includes:
- Business correspondence
- Customer service responses
- Professional networking emails
- Follow-up messages
- Apology or clarification emails
- Sales or marketing responses

## Core Principles

### 1. Understand the Context
- Read the original email carefully to understand the sender's intent
- Identify the purpose of the reply (acknowledge, respond, ask for clarification, etc.)
- Consider the relationship between sender and recipient
- Match the formality level of the original message

### 2. Structure for Clarity
- Open with a polite greeting
- Address the main points in order of importance
- Use clear, concise language
- Close with appropriate next steps or pleasantries
- Keep paragraphs short and scannable

### 3. Tone Considerations
- Adjust tone based on the relationship (formal vs. casual)
- Be respectful and professional at all times
- Consider cultural differences in communication styles
- Match or slightly elevate the formality of the original email
- Avoid jargon unless appropriate for the audience

### 4. Content Guidelines
- Be specific rather than vague
- Include all necessary information to avoid follow-up questions
- Use active voice when possible
- Proofread for grammar and spelling
- Ensure the email achieves its intended purpose
- Keep emails concise and under 150 words when possible
- Remove unnecessary spaces and extra line breaks
- Use shorter, more direct document titles when creating files

## Email Structure Template

### Opening
- Acknowledge receipt if appropriate ("Thank you for your email" or "Thanks for reaching out")
- Personalize greeting when possible

### Body
- Address main points in a logical order
- Use bullet points for multiple items
- Provide context when necessary
- Include relevant details or information requested

### Closing
- State next steps or expected action
- Offer additional help if appropriate
- Use appropriate sign-off ("Best regards," "Sincerely," etc.)

## Handling Special Situations

### Difficult Conversations
- Be empathetic and acknowledge concerns
- Focus on solutions rather than problems
- Take responsibility where appropriate
- Offer specific next steps

### Requests for Information
- Respond promptly and completely
- Organize information logically
- Include relevant context or explanations
- Offer additional assistance if needed

### Follow-ups
- Reference the previous conversation
- Provide updates or status information
- Reiterate next steps if applicable
- Set expectations for future communication

## Quality Checklist

Before sending an email, ensure it:
- [ ] Clearly addresses the sender's main points
- [ ] Uses appropriate tone for the relationship and context
- [ ] Is free of grammatical errors
- [ ] Includes all necessary information
- [ ] Has a clear purpose and call to action if needed
- [ ] Maintains professional standards
- [ ] Stays under 150 words for conciseness
- [ ] Contains no unnecessary spaces or extra line breaks
- [ ] Uses shorter, more direct document titles when creating files
